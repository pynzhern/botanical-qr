Hydrangea macrophylla — anatomical study

Bigleaf / mophead hydrangea. A schematic study of the real three-dimensional form — the showy sterile floret, the tiny fertile flower, the head architecture, the leaf, and the shrub habit — drawn to the measured botanical description, to ground the voxel-QR build (and fix v1's floating head + missing florets).

1 · The showy sterile floret (the thing you actually see)

Colour is pH-driven (Al³⁺ + delphinidin): blue (acidic) · purple (neutral) · pink (alkaline) · white cultivars; often all three within one head, plus cream-green when opening.

2 · The tiny fertile flower (hidden in a mophead)

In a mophead these are buried/absent beneath the showy sterile florets → for the QR model they don't matter. In a lacecap they form the granular fertile centre. Avg head ≈ 400 sterile + 200 fertile florets.

3 · Head — mophead vs lacecap (corymb / dichasial)

Head ≈ 9–20cm (avg 15). Built by dichasial branching — a corymb where outer/lower stalks are longer so all florets rise to ~one rounded surface. I'll model the iconic mophead.

4 · Leaf — opposite, decussate, coarsely serrate

5 · Whole plant — multi-stemmed mounded shrub

Woody deciduous shrub, 3–6ft tall & wide, rounded mound, freely branching; heads terminal on the shoots. (Real one is a bush; potted, we show a compact version.)
